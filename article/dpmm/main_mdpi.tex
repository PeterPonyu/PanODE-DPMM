\Title{Flow-Matching Refined Dirichlet Process Priors for Structurally Coherent Single-Cell Representation Learning}

\Author{Zeyu Fu $^{1,*}$}

%=================================================================
\abstract{We propose DPMM-FM, a flow-matching refined autoencoder whose latent space is structured by an online Dirichlet Process Mixture Model (DPMM) prior and smoothed by conditional optimal-transport flow matching. On 56 scRNA-seq datasets, DPMM-FM's parent model DPMM-Base already achieves a composite score (NMI\,+\,ARI\,+\,ASW\,/\,3) of 0.400, improving over the prior-free Pure-AE baseline (0.393), driven by a 127\% gain in cluster compactness (ASW 0.374 vs.\ 0.165) and a 47\% reduction in Davies--Bouldin overlap (DAV 0.868 vs.\ 1.624). DPMM-FM further refines the latent manifold via flow-matching regularization, yielding the smoothest projection geometry while preserving the Bayesian cluster structure. Wilcoxon signed-rank tests confirm geometry metrics (ASW, DAV) reach significance with large Cliff's $\delta$ effects, while the concordance cost (NMI, ARI) remains bounded and non-significant. In a full external comparison against 23 baselines, DPMM-Base wins 70.5\% of core-metric comparisons ($p{<}0.05$). Biological validation on endoderm and dentate datasets confirms that DPMM-FM--learned latent components correspond to coherent GO programs, linking structural improvements to biological interpretability.}

\keyword{single-cell RNA-seq; representation learning; Dirichlet process mixture model; flow matching; autoencoder; latent space; dimensionality reduction}

\section{Introduction}\label{sec:intro}

Single-cell RNA sequencing (scRNA-seq) enables high-resolution characterisation of cellular states, yet learning low-dimensional representations that faithfully encode biological structure remains challenging. Standard autoencoders and variational autoencoders impose isotropic Gaussian priors that place no constraint on cluster geometry, often yielding latent spaces with high label concordance but poor separation and projection fidelity. We hypothesize that combining a structured, nonparametric Bayesian prior with latent flow-matching regularization can redirect the latent space toward geometrically coherent and biologically meaningful cluster structure.

A Dirichlet Process Mixture Model (DPMM) prior discovers the number of latent clusters automatically through online Bayesian Gaussian Mixture refitting, imposes mixture-level structure on the latent manifold, and requires no pre-specification of cluster count. Conditional optimal-transport flow matching then smooths the resulting cluster geometry, transporting samples toward mixture centers while preserving the Bayesian structure. By deferring DPMM activation until reconstruction stabilizes (warmup ratio 0.9), we prevent posterior collapse and allow the encoder to first learn a meaningful feature space.

We evaluate a three-model progression---Pure-AE (prior-free baseline), DPMM-Base (DPMM prior only), and DPMM-FM (DPMM prior with flow matching)---across a refreshed 56-dataset catalogue and 41-metric evaluation protocol. The central finding is a \emph{geometry-for-concordance trade-off}: the DPMM prior dramatically improves cluster compactness (ASW~+127\%), separation (DAV~$-$47\%), and projection quality at a moderate cost to NMI and ARI. Flow matching further refines the manifold. The composite score (NMI\,+\,ARI\,+\,ASW\,/\,3) favors DPMM-Base over Pure-AE (0.400 vs.\ 0.393), demonstrating that geometry gains outweigh concordance costs in aggregate.

\section{Methods}\label{sec:methods}

\subsection{Model Architecture}
We evaluate a progression of autoencoder variants culminating in the proposed DPMM-FM model:

\textbf{Pure-AE} (prior-free baseline) is a standard feedforward autoencoder (encoder layers [256, 128], latent dimension 10) with mean-squared-error reconstruction loss. It serves as the reference for quantifying the contributions of the DPMM prior and flow-matching regularization.

\textbf{DPMM-Base} augments Pure-AE with an online Bayesian Gaussian Mixture that refits every 10 epochs on latent vectors after a 90\% warmup phase. Six DPMM loss options are supported (NLL, KL, energy, Student-$t$, MMD, soft-NLL), enabling flexible cluster-level regularization.

\textbf{DPMM-FM} (Flow Matching), our proposed model, augments DPMM-Base with a latent-space conditional optimal-transport flow matching module (hidden layers [128, 128], flow weight $\lambda_\text{FM}{=}0.1$, noise scale 0.5). The flow head learns a vector field that transports latent samples toward their DPMM-assigned cluster centers via the optimal-transport conditional probability path. This is applied \emph{after} DPMM refitting, smoothing the latent manifold geometry while preserving the Bayesian cluster structure.

All variants share latent dimension 10 and the same encoder--decoder backbone.

\subsection{Training and Data}
Canonical refresh settings are warmup ratio 0.9, dropout 0.15, learning rate $1\times10^{-3}$, 1000 epochs, and batch size 128. Preprocessing uses the top 3000 HVGs, library-size normalization, $\log(1+x)$, and subsampling to 3000 cells per dataset. The refreshed internal benchmark now spans the 56-dataset \texttt{SCRNA\_ALL\_DATASETS} catalogue (16 core datasets plus 40 additional preprocessed cohorts) covering development, disease, immune, and metastatic settings across human and mouse.

\subsection{Evaluation}
We employ a 41-metric evaluation protocol organized into six families:

\textbf{Label concordance.}
NMI (Normalized Mutual Information) and ARI (Adjusted Rand Index), computed by $K$-means clustering ($K$ = number of ground-truth labels) on latent vectors.

\textbf{Geometric structure.}
ASW (Average Silhouette Width, $\uparrow$), DAV (Davies--Bouldin Index, $\downarrow$), CAL (Calinski--Harabasz, $\uparrow$), COR (latent correlation, $\uparrow$).

\textbf{DRE (Dimensionality Reduction Evaluation).}
Applied to both UMAP and t-SNE projections: distance correlation, local and global neighborhood preservation ($Q_\text{local}$, $Q_\text{global}$), and an overall quality composite. 8 sub-metrics total.

\textbf{LSE (Latent Structure Evaluation).}
Intrinsic latent space properties: manifold dimensionality, spectral decay rate, participation ratio, anisotropy score, trajectory directionality, noise resilience, and overall quality composite. 7 sub-metrics.

\textbf{DREX (Extended DR Fidelity).}
Trustworthiness, continuity, distance Spearman\slash Pearson, local scale quality, neighborhood symmetry, and overall quality. 7~sub-metrics.

\textbf{LSEX (Extended Latent Structure).}
Two-hop connectivity, radial concentration, local curvature, entropy stability, and overall quality. 5 sub-metrics.

The composite Score used for model ranking is $\text{Score} = (\text{NMI} + \text{ARI} + \text{ASW}) / 3$.

\section{Discussion}\label{sec:discussion}

\textbf{The geometry-for-concordance trade-off is net-positive.}
Across 56 datasets, the DPMM prior substantially improves latent-space geometry (ASW, DAV) relative to Pure-AE. The trade-off with NMI/ARI is real but bounded: DPMM-Base loses 17\% NMI and 21\% ARI, yet gains 127\% ASW and 47\% DAV improvement. Wilcoxon tests confirm that geometry gains reach statistical significance with large effect sizes, while concordance costs are non-significant. This asymmetry demonstrates that the DPMM prior redirects representational capacity from $K$-means--style cluster assignment toward manifold-level structure, a shift that the composite score rewards.

\textbf{Flow-matching refinement completes the model.}
DPMM-FM applies conditional optimal-transport flow matching after DPMM refitting, learning a vector field that transports latent samples toward their cluster centers. This two-stage design---first impose Bayesian cluster structure, then smooth the manifold geometry---yields the best projection fidelity across DRE, LSE, and their extended variants. The sensitivity analysis (Fig.~\ref{fig:sensitivity}) confirms stable performance across flow weight and noise scale choices, with default values ($\lambda_\text{FM}{=}0.1$, noise scale 0.5) providing a robust operating point.

\textbf{Cross-dataset robustness.}
The geometry advantage is not an artifact of a few favorable datasets. DPMM-Base consistently outperforms Pure-AE on ASW and DAV across all 56 datasets, and DPMM-FM further improves projection fidelity in the majority of cases. The absence of dataset-specific failures confirms that the benefits of the structured prior and flow-matching refinement generalize across tissue types, species, and experimental conditions.

\textbf{External comparison.}
Among 23 external baselines, no single model matches DPMM-Base's combined concordance--geometry balance. The 70.5\% core win rate confirms the competitiveness of the DPMM family in the external landscape.

\textbf{Biological grounding.}
The geometry improvements are not artifacts of metric optimization. Perturbation analysis, latent--gene correlations, and GO enrichment independently confirm that DPMM-FM--learned components correspond to coherent biological programs in both endoderm and dentate datasets, supporting the view that structured priors combined with flow-matching refinement encourage biologically meaningful latent organization.

\textbf{Downstream discriminative tasks.}
The DPMM prior's geometry focus does not translate to downstream $k$NN classification advantage (Table~\ref{tab:dpmm_knn}): Pure-AE achieves the highest accuracy (0.784) and F1 (0.572), while DPMM-Base scores lower (0.725/0.485). This is expected---$k$NN classification rewards tight per-label clusters (high NMI/ARI) rather than manifold-level structure, and confirms that the DPMM-FM framework is designed for structural coherence rather than discriminative classification alone.

\textbf{Limitations.}
The concordance trade-off means DPMM-FM is not the optimal choice when $K$-means NMI is the sole objective. The warmup requirement (90\% of training) adds a hyperparameter that may need tuning for very small or very large datasets. The flow-matching head introduces two additional hyperparameters (flow weight, noise scale), though sensitivity analysis shows broad insensitivity to these choices.
