\Title{Flow-Matching Refined Dirichlet Process Priors for Structurally Coherent Single-Cell Representation Learning}

\Author{Zeyu Fu $^{1,*}$}

\AuthorNames{Zeyu Fu}

\address{%

%=================================================================
\abstract{We propose DPMM-FM, a flow-matching refined autoencoder whose latent space is structured by an online Dirichlet Process Mixture Model (DPMM) prior and smoothed by conditional optimal-transport flow matching. On 56 scRNA-seq datasets, DPMM-FM's parent model DPMM-Base achieves a composite score (NMI\,+\,ARI\,+\,ASW\,/\,3) of 0.400 versus the prior-free Pure-AE baseline (0.393), driven by a 127\% gain in cluster compactness (ASW 0.374 vs.\ 0.165) and a 47\% reduction in Davies--Bouldin overlap (DAV 0.868 vs.\ 1.624). DPMM-FM further refines the latent manifold via flow-matching regularization, achieving the highest projection fidelity (DRE, LSE, DREX) among all variants while preserving the Bayesian cluster structure. Wilcoxon signed-rank tests confirm geometry metrics (ASW, DAV) reach significance with large Cliff's $\delta$ effects, while the concordance cost (NMI, ARI) remains bounded and non-significant. In a full external comparison against 18 baselines, DPMM-Base wins 70.5\% of core-metric comparisons ($p{<}0.05$). Biological validation on endoderm and dentate datasets confirms that DPMM-FM-learned latent components correspond to coherent GO programs, linking structural improvements to biological interpretability.}

\keyword{single-cell RNA-seq; representation learning; Dirichlet process mixture model; flow matching; autoencoder; latent space; dimensionality reduction}

\section{Introduction}\label{sec:intro}

Single-cell RNA sequencing (scRNA-seq) enables high-resolution characterisation of cellular states~\cite{zheng2017massively,hwang2018single}, yet learning low-dimensional representations that faithfully encode biological structure remains challenging. Standard autoencoders and variational autoencoders (VAEs)~\cite{kingma2014auto} impose isotropic Gaussian priors that place no constraint on cluster geometry, often yielding latent spaces with high label concordance but poor separation and projection fidelity~\cite{lopez2018deep,gronbech2020scvae}. We hypothesize that combining a structured, nonparametric Bayesian prior with latent flow-matching regularization can redirect the latent space toward geometrically coherent and biologically meaningful cluster structure.

A Dirichlet Process Mixture Model (DPMM) prior~\cite{ferguson1973bayesian,neal2000markov} discovers the number of latent clusters automatically through online Bayesian Gaussian Mixture refitting~\cite{blei2006variational}, imposes mixture-level structure on the latent manifold, and requires no pre-specification of cluster count. Conditional optimal-transport flow matching~\cite{lipman2023flow,tong2024improving} then smooths the resulting cluster geometry, transporting samples toward mixture centers while preserving the Bayesian structure. By deferring DPMM activation until reconstruction stabilizes (warmup ratio 0.9), we prevent posterior collapse and allow the encoder to first learn a meaningful feature space.

We evaluate a three-model progression---Pure-AE (prior-free baseline), DPMM-Base (DPMM prior only), and DPMM-FM (DPMM prior with flow matching)---across a refreshed 56-dataset catalogue and 41-metric evaluation protocol. The central finding is a \emph{geometry-for-concordance trade-off}: the DPMM prior substantially improves cluster compactness (ASW~+127\%), separation (DAV~$-$47\%), and projection quality at a moderate cost to NMI and ARI. Flow matching further refines the manifold toward the highest projection fidelity. The composite score (NMI\,+\,ARI\,+\,ASW\,/\,3) favors DPMM-Base over Pure-AE (0.400 vs.\ 0.393), showing that geometry gains outweigh concordance costs in aggregate.

\section{Methods}\label{sec:methods}

\subsection{Model Architecture}
We evaluate a progression of autoencoder variants culminating in the proposed DPMM-FM model:

\textbf{Pure-AE} (prior-free baseline) is a standard feedforward autoencoder (encoder layers [256, 128], latent dimension 10) with mean-squared-error reconstruction loss~\cite{kingma2014auto}. It serves as the reference for quantifying the contributions of the DPMM prior and flow-matching regularization.

\textbf{DPMM-Base} augments Pure-AE with an online Bayesian Gaussian Mixture~\cite{blei2006variational,bishop2006pattern} that refits every 10 epochs on latent vectors after a 90\% warmup phase. Six DPMM loss options are supported (NLL, KL, energy, Student-$t$, MMD, soft-NLL), enabling flexible cluster-level regularization.

\textbf{DPMM-FM} (Flow Matching), our proposed model, augments DPMM-Base with a latent-space conditional optimal-transport flow matching module~\cite{lipman2023flow,tong2024improving} (hidden layers [128, 128], flow weight $\lambda_\text{FM}{=}0.1$, noise scale 0.5). The flow head learns a vector field that transports latent samples toward their DPMM-assigned cluster centers via the optimal-transport conditional probability path. This is applied \emph{after} DPMM refitting, smoothing the latent manifold geometry while preserving the Bayesian cluster structure.

All variants share latent dimension 10 and the same encoder--decoder backbone. Figure~\ref{fig:arch} illustrates the full DPMM-FM architecture.

\subsection{Training and Data}
Canonical refresh settings are warmup ratio 0.9, dropout 0.15, learning rate $1\times10^{-3}$, 1000 epochs, and batch size 128. Preprocessing uses the top 3000 highly variable genes (HVGs), library-size normalization, $\log(1+x)$, and subsampling to 3000 cells per dataset~\cite{wolf2018scanpy}. The refreshed internal benchmark spans the 56-dataset \texttt{SCRNA\_ALL\_DATASETS} catalogue (16 core datasets plus 40 additional preprocessed cohorts) covering development, disease, immune, and metastatic settings across human and mouse.

\subsection{Evaluation}
We employ a 41-metric evaluation protocol organized into six families:

\textbf{Label concordance.}
NMI (Normalized Mutual Information)~\cite{strehl2002cluster} and ARI (Adjusted Rand Index)~\cite{hubert1985comparing}, computed by $K$-means clustering ($K$ = number of ground-truth labels) on latent vectors.

\textbf{Geometric structure.}
ASW (Average Silhouette Width)~\cite{rousseeuw1987silhouettes}, DAV (Davies--Bouldin Index, $\downarrow$)~\cite{davies1979cluster}, CAL (Calinski--Harabasz, $\uparrow$)~\cite{calinski1974dendrite}, COR (latent correlation, $\uparrow$).

\textbf{DRE (Dimensionality Reduction Evaluation).}
Applied to both UMAP~\cite{mcinnes2018umap} and t-SNE~\cite{vandermaaten2008visualizing} projections: distance correlation, local and global neighborhood preservation ($Q_\text{local}$, $Q_\text{global}$), and an overall quality composite. 8 sub-metrics total.

\textbf{LSE (Latent Structure Evaluation).}
Intrinsic latent space properties: manifold dimensionality, spectral decay rate, participation ratio, anisotropy score, trajectory directionality, noise resilience, and overall quality composite. 7 sub-metrics.

\textbf{DREX (Extended DR Fidelity).}
Trustworthiness, continuity, distance Spearman\slash Pearson, local scale quality, neighborhood symmetry, and overall quality. 7~sub-metrics.

\textbf{LSEX (Extended Latent Structure).}
Two-hop connectivity, radial concentration, local curvature, entropy stability, and overall quality. 5 sub-metrics.

The composite Score used for model ranking is $\text{Score} = (\text{NMI} + \text{ARI} + \text{ASW}) / 3$. This metric weights concordance (NMI, ARI) at two-thirds and geometry (ASW) at one-third; projection fidelity metrics (DRE, LSE, DREX) are not captured by the composite.

The DPMM prior introduces a clear \emph{geometry-for-concordance trade-off}. Pure-AE achieves the highest NMI (0.609) and ARI (0.406), yet its geometry is weak (ASW 0.165, DAV 1.624). \section{Discussion}\label{sec:discussion}

\textbf{The geometry-for-concordance trade-off.}
The DPMM prior~\cite{ferguson1973bayesian} redirects representational capacity from $K$-means--style cluster assignment toward manifold-level structure. Across 56 datasets, DPMM-Base gains 127\% ASW~\cite{rousseeuw1987silhouettes} and 47\% DAV~\cite{davies1979cluster} improvement at a bounded and statistically non-significant NMI/ARI cost (Table~\ref{tab:dpmm_wilcoxon_core}). The composite score (0.400 vs.\ 0.393) reflects this net benefit, though the margin is modest because the composite weights concordance at two-thirds.

\textbf{Flow-matching refinement targets projection fidelity.}
DPMM-FM applies conditional optimal-transport flow matching~\cite{lipman2023flow,tong2024improving} after DPMM refitting. On the 8 core datasets, DPMM-FM achieves the highest DRE (0.751), LSE (0.695), and DREX (0.873) among all variants (Table~\ref{tab:dpmm_ablation_core}), at the cost of lower concordance and composite score relative to DPMM-Base. This positions DPMM-Base as the balanced choice for general use, and DPMM-FM as the variant optimized for applications requiring faithful low-dimensional projections---such as trajectory analysis and manifold visualization---where projection fidelity matters more than $K$-means label recovery. The sensitivity analysis (Fig.~\ref{fig:sensitivity}) confirms stable performance across flow weight and noise scale choices.

\textbf{External comparison.}
Among 18 external baselines---spanning classical VAE variants~\cite{kingma2014auto,higgins2017beta}, single-cell-specific models~\cite{lopez2018deep,cao2020searching,xiong2022online}, and deep clustering methods---no single model achieves comparable combined concordance--geometry balance to DPMM-Base. The 70.5\% core win rate (Table~\ref{tab:dpmm_external_winrate}) confirms this competitive positioning.

\textbf{Biological grounding.}
Perturbation analysis, latent--gene correlations, and GO enrichment~\cite{ashburner2000gene} on three representative datasets confirm that DPMM-FM-learned components correspond to coherent biological programs (Fig.~\ref{fig:bio}). Block-diagonal structure in the importance and correlation heatmaps indicates that individual latent dimensions capture distinct expression programs, and enriched GO terms align with known biology of each tissue context.

\textbf{Limitations.}
The concordance trade-off means DPMM-FM is not optimal when $K$-means NMI is the sole objective; DPMM-Base is preferable for balanced applications. The warmup requirement (90\% of training) and two additional flow-matching hyperparameters (flow weight, noise scale) add complexity, though sensitivity analysis shows broad robustness to these choices. The biological validation covers three datasets; broader validation across more tissue types would strengthen the generalizability claim.

\section{Conclusions}\label{sec:conclusions}
We introduced DPMM-FM, an autoencoder whose latent space is structured by an online Dirichlet Process Mixture Model prior~\cite{ferguson1973bayesian,neal2000markov} and refined by conditional optimal-transport flow matching~\cite{lipman2023flow}. Across 56 scRNA-seq datasets, the DPMM prior delivers a geometry-for-concordance trade-off: cluster compactness (ASW) improves by 127\% and Davies--Bouldin overlap drops by 47\%, at a bounded cost to NMI and ARI. DPMM-FM further extends this toward the highest projection fidelity among all variants. External comparison against 18 baselines confirms a 70.5\% core win rate for DPMM-Base, and biological validation on three datasets links the improved geometry to coherent gene-expression programs~\cite{ashburner2000gene}. Together, DPMM-Base and DPMM-FM offer complementary operating points---balanced concordance--geometry versus maximal projection fidelity---demonstrating that structured nonparametric priors combined with flow-matching regularization provide a principled route toward geometrically coherent single-cell representations.
